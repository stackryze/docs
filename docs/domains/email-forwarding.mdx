---
title: "Email forwarding"
description: "How forwarding from your Stackryze Domain to any inbox works, and what it does and doesn't do."
---

Every Stackryze Domain — under `indevs.in`, `sryze.cc`, `ryzedns.org`, or `nx.kg` — comes with email forwarding at `<anything>@<name>.<namespace>`. It's the simplest way to have a stable, project-specific email address without running a mail server.

## What forwarding is

A forwarded address receives mail at `<anything>@<name>.<namespace>` and delivers it to a destination inbox you control. The destination is a normal address on Gmail, Outlook, Fastmail, iCloud, ProtonMail, or any other provider.

In DNS terms, the address doesn't receive mail directly. Stackryze runs a forwarding MTA that accepts the message on your behalf, rewrites the envelope, and re-sends it to your real inbox.

## What it gives you

- A stable email address tied to your domain, not your ISP or current provider.
- The freedom to change destination inbox without telling anyone your new address.
- The ability to create unique per-project addresses (`hello@`, `support@`, `press@`, `noreply@`) that all land in the same place.
- Zero mail-server administration.

## What it doesn't do

Forwarding is not full email hosting:

- **No mailbox.** You can't log in to a `you@<name>.<namespace>` inbox. Mail is delivered to a third-party mailbox.
- **No sending from your domain.** You can't send mail *as* `you@<name>.<namespace>` from a forwarded address. For that, you need a real mail provider (Fastmail, ProtonMail, Google Workspace, etc.) with SMTP credentials.
- **No calendar, contacts, or storage.** It's an address, not a mail suite.
- **No IMAP/POP.** You access your mail at the destination, not at any Stackryze namespace.

## How it works under the hood

When you create a forwarded address, Stackryze adds the records that real mail servers need:

- An `MX` record pointing at the Stackryze forwarding MTA.
- An `SPF` (TXT) record authorizing the MTA to send mail on behalf of the domain.
- Optionally a `DMARC` (TXT) record so receivers can validate the chain.

These records are managed for you — you don't need to touch the DNS zone to enable forwarding.

## Sending mail from your domain (separate problem)

If you want to **send** mail *as* `you@<name>.<namespace>`, you need SMTP credentials from a real mail provider. The forwarding MTA only handles inbound mail.

Common pairings:

| Forwarding (receive) | Sending |
| --- | --- |
| Stackryze Domains | Fastmail / ProtonMail / Google Workspace |

When you switch the destination of a forward, the destination changes but the address doesn't — people can keep sending to `you@<name>.<namespace>` and you'll still get it.

## Limits and quotas

- **Forwarding addresses per domain:** 50 by default. Need more? Email [support@stackryze.com](mailto:support@stackryze.com).
- **Forwarding rate limits** apply on burst sends to fight spam; if a sender hits the limit, they're rate-limited the same way any MTA would rate-limit them.
- **Attachment size:** standard SMTP limits apply (typically 25 MB).
- **Greylisting:** on first contact, the MTA may temporarily reject mail and retry. This is normal SMTP behavior and most legitimate senders handle it automatically.

## Abuse handling

Forwarding is provided to humans, not to bulk senders. If a forwarded address starts receiving spam at volume, Stackryze applies the same rate limits and abuse controls any provider would. Repeated abuse can result in the forward being disabled; see [Security and abuse](/docs/domains/security-and-abuse).
